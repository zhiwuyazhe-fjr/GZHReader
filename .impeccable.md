## Design Context

### Users
GZHReader is primarily for individual knowledge workers who subscribe to many WeChat public accounts and want a calmer way to turn scattered daily reading into a durable personal briefing. They open the app to check whether today's briefing is ready, trigger a run, glance at service health, and move back into reading or writing.

### Brand Personality
Quiet, editorial, trustworthy. The interface should feel like a personal editor's desk rather than a systems console. The emotional goal is calm confidence: the app should feel composed, capable, and quietly premium without looking flashy or overly technical.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is "paper editorial studio". The primary mode is a warm light theme with strong typography, generous whitespace, restrained color, and content-first hierarchy. A matching dark theme should preserve the same editorial sensibility rather than switching to neon or dashboard aesthetics. The app should not look like a DevOps console, generic SaaS admin, or sci-fi AI product.

### Design Principles
1. Put today's briefing first. The homepage should answer "is today's reading ready?" before anything else.
2. Keep operations calm. Status, setup gaps, and recovery flows should be understandable without step-by-step lockouts.
3. Favor editorial structure over control-panel density. Use hierarchy, spacing, and typography instead of repeated cards and badges.
4. Hide complexity behind dedicated settings. The homepage stays focused on reading and action, while setup and advanced controls live on a settings page.
5. Make the product feel self-contained. Users should not need to understand Docker, MySQL, or upstream service internals to use the app.
